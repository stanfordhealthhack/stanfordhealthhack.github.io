About the experiment

Healthcare needs more unusual teams.

Stanford Health Hack brings students, clinicians, engineers, designers, researchers, business thinkers, and digital health leaders together to tackle pressing healthcare challenges with AI and emerging technology.

The weekend is built to do more than produce demos. It is a launchpad for relationships, viable Stanford innovations, and ideas that can keep moving toward real-world care long after the final pitch.

Challenge tracks

Choose a systemto shake up.

01 / CLINICAL AI

AI-Powered Clinical Tools

What to build: Develop agentic AI assistants, diagnostic copilots, and smart triage systems that empower clinicians and streamline hospital workflows.

02 / REMOTE CARE

Untethered Care: Mobile & Remote Diagnostics

What to build: Design device and smartphone-linked solutions, offline-capable apps, and portable tech that bring high-quality diagnostic power to remote areas and mobile clinics.

03 / FEMTECH

FemTech & Women’s Health Innovation

What to build: Create targeted digital health and AI solutions addressing reproductive health, maternal care, and long-overlooked gaps in women's wellness.

04 / PEDIATRICS

NextGen Care: Pediatrics & Children’s Health

What to build: Innovate pediatric-specific AI tools, developmental trackers, and tech tailored to the unique physical and emotional needs of younger populations.

05 / LONGEVITY

Longevity & Consumer Health

What to build: Build proactive consumer applications focused on preventive wellness, fitness optimization, and bio-tracking to help people live longer, healthier lives.

01

Applying & participation

Stanford Health Hack is a fast-paced, collaborative weekend where participants come together to tackle real healthcare challenges. You will form interdisciplinary teams, build prototypes, test ideas, learn from mentors, and create solutions that could improve the future of health and medicine.
Any Stanford affiliate with an active SUNet ID is eligible to apply.
Not at all. Great teams need more than programmers. We welcome clinicians, researchers, designers, engineers, entrepreneurs, students, storytellers, operators, and anyone passionate about improving healthcare.
All experience levels are welcome, from first-time hackers to seasoned builders. Whether this is your first hackathon or your tenth, mentors, workshops, and resources will help you make the most of the weekend.
No. Many participants arrive without a team. We will offer opportunities to meet potential teammates before and during the event, and participants may also choose to work independently.
Teams may have up to five participants.
Complete the application form. If selected, you will receive a confirmation and calendar invitation with event details. Confirm your participation by accepting the calendar invitation.
Admission details will be shared soon.
Applications will be reviewed after the submission deadline. Accepted participants will receive a confirmation email and calendar invitation with event details, venue information, and next steps.
Waitlist and walk-in details will be shared soon.
Teams are welcome to discuss ideas, explore problem areas, and form teams before the event. However, all project development and implementation must take place during the official hackathon.
Nothing. Participation is completely free thanks to the generous support of Stanford Medicine Catalyst and Stanford's Center for Digital Health.

No. The venue will close each evening, and participants will need to arrange their own accommodations. We encourage everyone to get some rest and return refreshed the next day.
